TRICOLORE: a ECIMA la nuova eMTB firmata Moto Parilla

L’azienda di Reggio Emilia continua a stupire il mondo della due ruote presentando l’innovativa e-MTB che strizza l’occhio alle moto e rivoluziona il concetto di gravity.

Un po’ moto e un po’ bici. Questa è Tricolore, la neonata e-MTB di Moto Parilla che sceglie EICMA 2022 per togliere il velo e presentarla al mondo degli appassionati delle due ruote. Dopo due anni di ricerca e sviluppo l’azienda della Motor Valley emiliana stravolge gli schemi del mondo enduro. Una bicicletta rivoluzionaria con linee e accorgimenti che strizzano l’occhio alle sorelle più grandi e massicce che compongono l’universo delle moto. La scelta di ECIMA, quale palcoscenico per la presentazione ufficiale, è conseguenza ovvia.

Completamente progettata, sviluppata e prodotta in Italia, Tricolore adotta materiali di altissima qualità quali la fibra di carbonio e l’alluminio aerospaziale. Moto Parilla presta anche attenzione all’ambiente. La maggior parte della componentistica viene realizzata entro un raggio di 50 chilometri dalla sede. Inoltre, per rendere più sostenibile il veicolo e dare un impulso alla transizione ecologica, diverse parti vengono prodotte in fibra di canapa o lino, totalmente riciclabili a fine vita e capaci di ridurre a zero l’impatto sull’ambiente. 

Tre componenti principali di Tricolore: telaio centrale, forcellone e telaietto reggisella. Le parti sono accoppiate con soluzioni tecnologiche all’avanguardia e danno vita a un mezzo che abbandona la classica struttura triangolare delle biciclette.

Tricolore è un mezzo versatile ideale sia nelle lunghe escursioni attraverso ogni terreno e in qualsiasi condizione meteorologica che nei single track più tecnici e difficoltosi. L’autonomia, la potenza e la coppia del sistema powertrain permetteranno di pedalare per molti chilometri e sulle salite più ripide. 

Tricolore, così come Carbon, e-bike rivoluzionaria dal design unico, e Trilix, city foldable e-bike dallo stile inconfondibile, è totalmente customizzabile per poter esprimere al meglio la propria personalità.

Moto Parilla Tricolore, the presentation at EICMA (3/3)

Here we are at the latest episode inside Tricolore, the new e-MTB developed by Moto Parilla that will debut at EICMA in November this year. Below is the latest set of details describing the new features and innovations that this new pedal-assist mountain bike will bring to the market.

COVER COMPARTMENT

At the heart of Tricolore will be a watertight internal compartment, carved into the frame and accessible by lock or quick release, where an emergency kit, repair tools or other personal items can be stored. In the same compartment will be an aluminum plate that will contain the data and version of one’s bike.

CARBON FIBER MONOCOQUE FRAME

Tricolore’s distinctive carbon fiber monocoque frame will be completely different from the standard triangular geometries belonging to the cycling industry. The ribs on the frame will have a reinforcing function and decrease the adhesion of mud on the frame. In this part, too, it will be possible to install a water bottle holder thanks to the grafts on the body.

FRONT LIGHTING

The front lighting system will be integrated inside Tricolore’s carbon fiber monocoque frame. The top cover, made of hemp fiber, will allow the cables to be routed inside the frame itself. A recess will be made in the frame so as to accommodate an aluminum plate with the Tricolor logo.

See you at EICMA for the official presentation.

The journey inside Tricolore, Moto Parilla’s e-MTB, continues (2/3)

The journey inside Tricolore, the new e-MTB developed by Moto Parilla that will debut at EICMAin November this year, continues. Here is a new set of details describing the new features and innovations that this new pedal-assist mountain bike will bring to the market.

HEMP COVER

One of the main features of Tricolore will be the presence of a cover system made of hemp fiber, which is also totally recyclable because the fiber used will be organic like the resin with which it is impregnated. This is intended to underscore the company’s commitment to trying to follow the ecological transition, so it will be necessary to find substitutable, less polluting materials for vehicles throughout the cycling and motorcycling industry.

The top cover will have an airtight seal to prevent the entry of fluids or dirt but at the same time allow the passage of cables inside the chassis. The holes on it will allow the mounting of additional accessories, such as water bottles or additional displays, and the quick release or lock closure system will allow maximum convenience in inserting and removing the battery.

INNOVATIVE SWINGARM

Tricolore’s swingarm, which is clearly motorcycling in nature, was developed after a study that lasted nearly two years. Thanks to a bold and extremely innovative choice, it will be single bilateral arm and made entirely of carbon fiber. In addition, it will be reinforced with aluminum inserts in the areas of wheel coupling, rear brake caliper and suspension system, to ensure greater stability and strength.

The wide channel will provide the ability to fit tires up to 3.5″ and will allow excellent mud drainage under all circumstances. The transmission system will be 12-speed, and the Flip-Chip system will allow easy variation of the wheelbase.

LIGHTING SYSTEM

The futuristic rear lighting system will be integrated into the seatpost frame and powered by the powertrain battery. Control of the entire system directly from the handlebar controller will be allowed, ensuring ease of use and performance in both daytime and night trials.

Inside TRICOLORE, the new MTB from Moto Parilla (1/3)

While waiting for EICMA, the international bicycle and motorbike trade fair to be held in Milan in November, over the next few weeks we will take you on a tour of each of the details that characterise the Tricolore, the new e-mtb made by Moto Parilla.

In the new Tricolore, the rear suspension system will be made up of a rocker arm, connecting rod and shock absorber to ensure great vehicle responsiveness. The linkages, completely made of Ergal 7075 aluminium, will be machined from billet. The entire mechanism will be protected by a specially designed rear mudguard made of polycarbonate.

In addition, the seatpost frame, also of a motorbike nature, will be detached from the frame and made entirely of carbon fibre. The modular attachment system of the seatpost and shock absorber to the frame will consist of lightened aluminium mesh plates that will make Tricolore not only extremely strong but also cutting-edge in terms of design.

The Tricolore’s engine casing will be made of carbon kevlar fibre for sensational impact resistance and will feature an integrated cooling system by means of a special design. In order to make the vehicle modular, a universal engine mounting system was designed using plates made from billet 7075 aluminium. The engine will be thePolini E-P3+ MX, capable of delivering 250W of power, a 600W peak and 90 Nm of torque. Recharging the battery will be facilitated by the presence of its frame connection.

TRICOLORE: the Italian eMTB revolution designed by Moto Parilla

This is what Moto Parilla‘s new electric MTB will look like, a real revolution in the world of mountain bikes, combining design and innovation. It will be an hybrid of the motorcycle and bike, with unique technical features. First on the road at EICMA2022.

Reggio Emilia-based innovative startup, Moto Parilla, never ceases to amaze, after ‘Carbon,’ an aggressively designed e-bike that revolutionized the eBike world, and Trilix, a city eBike prized for its unmistakable style.

Today, after two years of research and development, Moto Parilla proudly presents its new TRICOLORE, the revolutionary enduro e-MTB that will be officially unveiled at EICMA 2022 in November. The world’s most important exhibition space was not a random choice, as TRICOLORE will evidence its strong motorcycle influences, including the monocoque frame, the bilateral single-arm swingarm (Single-Pivot), an interchangeable seat frame for different uses, and the geometries peculiar to the motorcycle world that go beyond the classic triangular shapes.

This is a modular vehicle that allows for varying bike geometries by taking advantage of several accordants developed by Moto Parilla that enable it to fully meet customer needs on off-road trails or urban routes. In fact, the possibility of mounting different seat-post frames, the variability of suspension fulcrums, the interchangeability of different drivetrain systems, and the possibility of equipping the vehicle with powertrains providing an excess of 750 watts, make TRICOLORE versatile and performing anywhere.

Moto Parilla has always mirrored the ‘Made in Italy‘ image. For this reason, the monocoque frame, swing-arm and all other components will be manufactured entirely in Italy, within an approximate radius of 50 km from the Emilian headquarters. Refined materials such as carbon fiber, hemp fiber and aerospace aluminum will be used to ensure optimal performance, lightness and durability. The power-train will also be Italian, thanks to the agreement with Polini, which will supply the ‘Polini EP3 250 Watt‘ model capable of ensuring complete reliability and power needed to reach a peak of 500 Watts and 90 Nm of torque. The bike is equipped with an 880 Watt-hour battery capable of providing a range of over 100km. The braking system, suspension and all other commercial components will vary according to the model chosen and the destined use.

High attention will also be given to ecological transition by using less polluting or recyclable materials. In particular, hemp fiber, which will be used for the construction of the non-structural components, will be totally recyclable thanks to special natural resins used in the production process that will make it possible for the parts to be completely recycled at the end of their life.

TRICOLOREis a highly innovative product in the world of electric vehicles, and constitutes the specific meeting point between the worlds of motorcycles and bicycles.

MOTO PARILLA HOMAGES THE ‘GIRO D’ITALIA’

The ‘Giro d’Italia’ will arrive tomorrow Wednesday 18 May in Reggio Emilia, home of ‘Moto Parilla’, starting from Parma on Thursday. To pay homage to the passage of the “Corsa Rosa”, here is a ‘Carbon’ in the colors of the Giro, made available to the Bardiani CSF Faizanè team.

Moto Parilla, a Reggio Emilia-based company specializing in the production of artisanal e-bikes of the highest level in terms of design and technique, 100% Made in Italy, has decided to pay tribute to the passage of the Giro d’Italia in the Emilian land.

The eleventh stage will arrive in Reggio Emilia, home of ‘Moto Parilla’, on 18 May, to leave the following day from nearby Parma.

The company led by Filippo Beltrami has therefore decided to pay homage to the Corsa Rosa, creating a ‘Carbon‘ with the typical colors of the tour, pink indeed, producing a unique example of e-bike, which will be made available to the Bardiani-CSF-Faizanè team from the arrival of the stage until the restart the following day.

“This year we decided to experience the emotions of the Giro d’Italia up close – said Filippo Beltrami, founder of Moto Parilla –for this reason we wanted to pay homage to this wonderful race by creating a unique bike, as unique is the Corsa Rosa to which we are all fond of, together with the friends of Bardiani-CSF-Faizanè engaged as protagonists in the Giro d’Italia “.
